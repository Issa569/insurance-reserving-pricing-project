# Insurance Reserving & Ratemaking

## Project Overview

In this project, we analyze the performance of a multi-line insurance portfolio across Medical, Motor, and Property business for underwriting years 2021 to 2025.

The analysis focuses on reserve estimation, claim development behavior, pricing adequacy, and portfolio performance using actuarial reserving and ratemaking techniques. The project was completed using Excel and includes development triangles, Chain Ladder analysis, pricing indications, and portfolio interpretation.

## Main Objectives

- Review underwriting performance across multiple lines of business
- Analyze claim frequency, severity, and loss cost trends
- Build monthly incurred development triangles
- Apply the incurred Chain Ladder method
- Estimate ultimate losses and reserve requirements
- Evaluate pricing adequacy for the upcoming renewal period
- Interpret portfolio volatility and development uncertainty

## Methods Used

- Incurred Chain Ladder Method
- Development Triangle Analysis
- Age-to-Age Development Factors
- Loss Ratio Method
- Loss Cost Method
- Frequency-Severity Analysis
- Reserve Estimation (IBNR & Case Reserves)

## Key Findings

- The portfolio remained relatively stable from 2021 to 2023 before experiencing heavier losses in 2024.
- The Medical line showed the highest volatility due to several higher-severity claims.
- Total reserve requirements were estimated at approximately \$23,122.
- Technical pricing indications suggested a moderate rate decrease, though the final recommendation remained more conservative due to credibility concerns and portfolio size.
